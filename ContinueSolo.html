Expedition, 1808

Story

With the Corps now completely mounted on horseback you are now covering vast amounts of ground everyday. You still have not sighted anything that resembles a western passage; in fact the Corps is more concerned with moving westward than anything else at this moment. After many weeks of hard riding and very little incidents of note, you begin noticing sea birds. The coast is only moments away. Soon you can smell the ocean, and after a momentary climb to the top of a nearby hill you catch first sight of the Pacific Ocean. You have done it. The Frontier Corps of the U.A.R has traversed the entire continent. This disappointment of not finding the western passage wears of with the realization that you are the first group of men to cross the country. The task you are now confronted with is establishing a permanent U.A.R presence on the west coast. As the men construct the fort, you think about the western passage. Part of you wants to lead a separate expedition to find it on the way back east, while part of you wishes to stay on the west coast and help establish relations with local tribes.

CHOICES

  • You ask for volunteers for the return trip home including a search for the western passage to your surprise a sizeable number of men volunteer for the expedition. You take whatever provisions you can and begin riding North. Because you are only packing a small amount of supplies you will not be able to construct forts. If you do not find the western passage within two months you will have to begin the ride to the winter fort in order to make it through the winter.
  • You decide to stay on the west coast and help establish diplomatic relations with the Natives living on the Pacific seaboard. You feel like you would better serve your country helping establish positive ties with the local populace. These Natives were technically citizens of the U.A.R now so you begin work on a census for the new territories. You also believe that if there is a western passage. The Natives are likely to know about it and it would be far easier to find with local Native help.
