Expedition, 1808

Story

The year is 1808 and the Unified American Republics just acquired a large cession of land from the Empire of France. The territory is one of unprecedented size and effectively doubles the size of the U.A.R's continental holdings. This land is especially important because there is legend of a western passage: a collection of rivers that connects the mighty Atlantic in the east to the vast Pacific on the west. This passage would allow the U.A.R to potentially avoid the long costly trip around the horn of South America. The President of the U.A.R just announced that an expedition will depart to chart the newly acquired territory and potentially discover the western passage. As a member of the Frontier Corps you will be participating in this journey. The commanding officer of the Frontier Corps is Captain Clark Lewis. A week before the Corps heads out Captain Lewis asks if you would be his second in command for the journey.

CHOICES

  • You happily accept Captain Lewis's offer. As the second in command you will be mostly in charge of day-to-day decisions while Captain Lewis focuses on helping the cartographers and establishing diplomatic relations with the natives. He tells you of an important meeting the next morning. You return home and get some sleep as the expedition departs in two days and there are no beds on the frontier.
  • You explain to Captain Lewis that you are greatly honored that he would consider you for the position. The second in command is commonly elected by the men in order to act as a bridge between the men and the Captain. You ask Captain Lewis to put it to a vote. He agrees to allow the men of the Corps to elect the second in command at tomorrows meeting.
