Expedition, 1808

Story

After deciding to leave the hostile Natives alone, they ceased to bother the men of the Frontier Corps. Of course the men were still afraid to enter the woods to trap for food, so the result was a long hungry winter. So hungry in fact that Captain Lewis thought of abandoning the expedition. In addition to the issues already faced by the lack of food, a small outbreak of a deadly fever occurred. In all about 30 men of the Frontier Corps perish during the winter. It was at this point that we learned of the fate of the hostile Native tribe. They were eventually wiped out during the winter by another local tribe called the Oar people. When spring began the Oar began sending envoys to the Corps fort, the Oar made it clear that they were friendly and willing to trade. The Oar had captured large quantities of rifles from the vanquished tribe. They offer to contribute men for the expedition in exchange for your remaining ammunition. Accepting the men will spread supplies thin, but the advantage of Native guides may be beneficial to the Corps. The morning of the decision Captain Lewis and a few others desert the expedition. The Oar people offers 30 men for your use, as the Frontier Corps commander the decision is yours.

CHOICES

  • You decide to welcome the 30 Natives into the Frontier Corps. You figure the extra men will be able to provide for themselves, as they are Natives. Also the Oar claim to know the western passage, which is what the Corps is searching for. The extra men depart the fort with your remaining men. This integration of Natives and U.A.R citizens is unprecedented, you hope this will eventually lead to less future conflicts with tribes that lie ahead.
  • You decide that even though the Natives have extensive knowledge of the upcoming terrain, the burden of an additional 30 men will ultimately slow the expedition to the point that they may have to spend another winter forted up before they reach the Pacific Ocean. Also weighing on your mind is the thought that these Natives could simply abandon the Corps if the going gets too tough. To have 30 men abandon during the middle of a trek would be disastrous. Plus with the reduced number of men, every member of the Corps will now be on horseback.
