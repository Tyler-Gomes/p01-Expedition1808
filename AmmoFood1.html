Expedition, 1808

Story

The next day the men of the Frontier Corps are gathering their final supplies for the voyage. Each man is to pack 60 pounds of gear and food over the entire duration of the trip. This weight does not include 10-pound rifle each man must carry. Some men shorten the barrel in order to save some weight and to carry a few more rounds of ammunition. The territory the Corps is trekking through is considered dangerous for many reasons but the threat of Native ambushes weighs the heaviest on everyoneâ€™s mind. The men want to carry as much ammunition as possible, however Captain Lewis fears the men will carry less food if they bring more ammo. Starving men could be a far worse situation than any Native ambush. He tasks you with making the decision on how much of which provision to bring more of: ammunition or food?

CHOICES

  • Carrying less food and more ammunition makes the most sense to you. Ammunition is non-perishable and can be utilized for much more, plus it gives the men more peace of mind. You reason that because the Frontier Corps is filled with former trappers foraging for food would not be too difficult for the Corps to handle. You inform Captain Lewis of your choice and he announces to the men that they can requisition double the ammo. The expedition begins tomorrow.
  • The expedition will go no further than its provisions allow it. The saying goes that an army marches on its stomach and this Corps is no different. The Natives will not have the chance to ambush the men if the Corps starves first. You reason that during the winter it will be extremely difficult to forage for food, and using ammunition to hunt for food is just going to burn through ammo while constantly alerting Natives to the Corps position. You tell Lewis of your opinion and he passes word to the men. With extra food in pack the men ready themselves for departure the next morning.
